UAE PEPPOL DCTCE
5-Corner (C5) Model — Step-by-Step Flow
PINT-AE rides on the Peppol Decentralised Continuous Transaction Control and Exchange model. Five corners. Both C2 and C3 report Tax Data Documents to C5 (FTA) — the dual-reporting model is what gives the regulator independent visibility on each side.
Step 1 of 12
STEP 1 — INVOICE SUBMISSION
C1 submits invoice to C2
The supplier's source system (ERP, core banking, PAS, or a manual upload) hands the invoice to ClearTax — sitting at C2 — over a REST API. Synchronous call.

TDS (Tax Data Status, TDT-006 = 'F') is the document type used when validation fails — distinct from a normal TDD. Used only on the rejection path.

MLS — Application Response codes

CodeStatusMeaning
ABAcknowledgedReceiver AP has the message; processing in progress.
APAcceptedValidated and delivered to C4.
RERejectedValidation failed. Carries a reason code. Also reported to C5.

MLS — Status reason codes

CodeMeaning
SVSchema validation failure (XSD)
BVBusiness validation failure (Schematron)
DLDelivered to C4
DFDelivery failed (could not reach C4)
NRNot routable (no valid endpoint)

Multiple MLS messages can be sent for the same invoice — typical sequence is AB first, then AP+DL on success or RE+SV/BV on failure.

Retries at scale — what we run

FailureBehaviour

MLS not received within SLAC2 polls; escalate as NOT_DELIVERED if still pending after threshold.
Orphan / in-flight envelopesHourly reconciliation worker sweeps to catch silent losses.

Two things that catch people out vs the simpler 4-corner Peppol model: (1) In UAE DCTCE, both C2 and C3 submit TDDs to C5 — sender side and receiver side, independently. (2) The TDD has three management functions: submit, replace, withdraw. When MLS = RE arrives, C2's earlier positive TDD at C5 has to be closed via a TDD withdraw. The negative outcome at C3 is reported via a TDS (Tax Data Status, TDT-006 = 'F'), not a TDD.
